# Ethical computer engineering and the power within
In taking up this field of study, I knew what was out for me. Being bitten by the radioactive spider of computer engineering comes great power and with great power comes great responsibility. In an exponentially expanding technological world with tech touching almost every end of the planet there are times where we have to know where to put our foot down and stand up for what is right. As an engineer, people have to understand that although it's selfish, "the developer is always right."

# NASA's Challenger
One instance in history is of an engineer's ethics being challenged is during the launch of NASA's Challenger rocket. When preparing to launch, the weather was cold and an engineer knew that the conditions were not correct. When consulting a manager, the manager told him "take off your engineering hat and put on your management hat." From a managerial standpoint they needed to make this happen. The rocket was not proven unsafe in these conditions. The engineer conformed with the managers call and the incident happened. The O-rings of NASA's Challenger failed at the cost of astronaut's lives. Instead of thinking that it wasn't proven unsafe, they should have taken extra precaution to make sure it was completely safe. Learning this lesson, as an engineers we need to know when to say stop. We will know the extent of our work.

# Privacy and software development
One big thing we need to take into account with ethics on the software development side of things is privacy. With many websites having databreaches and losing peoples personal information it's important to understand how to handle sensitive data. Wherever you go on the internet, you leave an internet footprint, but in an online media driven society it's almost essential to live. Instead of worrying about leaving your footprint on too many internet beaches, as a software developer we must worry about how to keep these beaches safe to walk on.
